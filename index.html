Red Flags & Problems
⚠ Personal Development is a View Graveyard
90 videos (32% of ALL content) generating only 13.1M views (7.6% of views). Avg just 145K/video. Solo rant-style videos like "I Feel Like Nobody Likes Me", "BREAKUPS: Solved" get under 50K. This content costs production time but delivers almost zero ROI.
⚠ 33% of videos have under 100K views
94 out of 284 videos are below 100K views. Almost all are Solo/Personal Development content. The channel has a massive long-tail problem — bottom 50 videos avg under 30K views.
⚠ Business category underperforming vs effort
14 Business videos with only 162K avg views. Despite big names like Aman Gupta (boAt), Sanjeev Bikhchandani, Grant Cardone — none crossed 1M. Business content has poor viral coefficient on this channel.
Untapped Opportunities
💡 Crime & Dark is Massively Underinvested
Only 4 videos but 1.1M avg views — the highest ROI category after Comedy. Kolkata Doctor case got 3.2M. Crime/dark truth/scam content consistently viral on Indian YouTube. 10x more Crime content = potential 10M+ additional views per month.
💡 Comedians from OTT/Stand-up are Goldmines
Every comedian episode crosses 2M+ views. Samay Raina (10.8M), Vir Das (1.7M), Karunesh Talwar, Akaash Singh all performed well. India's OTT boom is creating new comedian stars weekly — Prakhar should book them before they get too expensive.
💡 Sequel episodes work — repeat Aarush Laila formula
Aarush Laila Ep1 got 11.6M, Ep2 got 8.8M. Ravi Gupta Ep1 got 8.2M, Ep2 got 8.0M. Sequel episodes retain nearly full viewership — far more efficient than finding new guests. More sequels with proven performers = guaranteed high views.
💡 Military & Defence is punching above its weight
Only 10 videos but 495K avg views and very high emotional engagement. Col. Shivender Kanwar (1.1M), GD Bakshi, Kargil heroes all performed well. Post-Operation Sindoor, military content appetite in India is at all-time high.

Final Verdict — What This Channel Really Is

Prakhar Gupta's channel is fundamentally a Comedy-led guest podcast that has accidentally built a large Personal Development library. The top 7 videos are all comedians or entertainers — they account for 57M views alone. The channel's identity is clearest when Prakhar sits with a funny, controversial, or emotionally raw guest.

The biggest strategic mistake is producing 90 Personal Development solo videos that average only 145K views each. That's 90 videos × production time for the same output as 5 Comedy episodes. The audience follows Prakhar for his conversations, not his lectures.

The single biggest opportunity: book more comedians + crime/dark truth content + sequel episodes with proven guests. If the next 20 videos follow the Comedy/Crime/Entertainment pattern instead of Personal Development, the channel could realistically cross 300M total views by end of 2026.
